# EcoForge Weekly Operating Rhythm & Maintenance Checklist

**Version**: 1.6 – March 22, 2026

**Purpose**: Mid-level cadence for 1–2 operators to tune, maintain, and optimize the system weekly. Prevents small issues from compounding, builds momentum toward monthly goals, and ensures data/trends are captured. Scalable from Container 1 to Premium Hub nodes.

### Core Weekly Rhythm (Total ~45–90 minutes, spread across 1–2 sessions)
- **System Tune & Balance** (20–30 min)  
  - Review last 7 days of logged data (pH, DO, temp, ammonia trends)  
  - Adjust feed rate if FCR drifting (>1.8 target)  
  - Thin duckweed mat if >80% coverage (harvest 30–60%)  
  - Check biofloc color/consistency (coffee-brown ideal; adjust carbon if gray/white)  
  - Test worm tea EC and dilute to 0.5–1.5 mS/cm for foliar/feed use  

- **Component Maintenance** (20–40 min)  
  - Clean swirl settler / radial flow settler screens  
  - Flush/inspect IBC media beds or NFT channels for clogs  
  - Top-up vermicompost bins (maintain 50:50 greens:browns ratio; add bedding if shrinking)  
  - Inspect biogas digester: stir slurry, check pH 6.5–7.5, record gas yield/pressure  
  - Prune/harvest Mars bay greens (lettuce, herbs, kale — target 20–40% weekly yield)  
  - Clean solar panels and check battery SOC trends  

- **Data & Observation Logging** (10 min)  
  - Record weekly averages: temp, DO, pH, ammonia, nitrite, TSS  
  - Note visual health: fish behavior, plant vigor, root zone clarity  
  - Update tilapia cohort weight estimate or sample weigh-in  
  - Flag any anomalies for monthly deep-dive  

### Weekly Optimization Focus (Rotate or prioritize based on phase)
- Week 1: Water quality deep-check + carbon dosing calibration  
- Week 2: Vermi & biochar harvest/prep  
- Week 3: Energy system audit (solar yield, geothermal stability, biogas output)  
- Week 4: Yield & sales preview (estimate surplus duckweed, greens, castings)  

### Troubleshooting Flags ⚠️ (Act within 24–48 hours)
- pH creeping >7.8 or <6.5 → buffer with dolomite or vinegar/carbon  
- Ammonia >0.75 ppm → boost duckweed/carbon input, reduce feed 20% temporarily  
- DO dipping <4.5 mg/L → increase aeration runtime, check pump/air stones  
- Duckweed stalling (no doubling in 3–4 days) → thin aggressively, check nutrients/light  
- Biofloc crash (foul odor) → add aeration, reduce feed, dose carbon heavily  

### Success Thresholds (End of Week)
- Ammonia average <0.5 ppm (no spikes >1.0)  
- DO consistently >5 mg/L  
- Duckweed mat doubling observed or stable thin coverage  
- Greens harvest ≥1–2 kg (Mars bay)  
- Biogas production stable or increasing  
- No new fish stress signs (flashing, gasping, isolation)
