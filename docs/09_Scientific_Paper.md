# EcoForge Scientific Paper

**Title**  
Hybrid Aquaponics–Vermiponics–Biofloc System for Closed-Loop Off-Grid Abundance and Bioregenerative Life Support Analogs: Design, Performance, and Implications for Mars ECLSS  

**Version**: 1.1 – March 22, 2026

**Abstract**  
Bioregenerative life support systems remain a critical bottleneck for long-duration space missions due to incomplete nutrient/water closure and system complexity. We present EcoForge, a hybrid platform integrating Nile × Blue hybrid tilapia aquaponics, Eisenia fetida vermiponics, multi-strain biofloc (Bacillus subtilis/licheniformis + Nitrosomonas europaea/Nitrobacter winogradskyi + Rhodopseudomonas palustris), Lemna minor duckweed, biogas CO₂ enrichment, and renewable energy (micro-hydro, solar, geothermal GSHP). The system achieves **60–85% practical closure** in water, nitrogen, and oxygen cycles, produces 70–210 lb protein and 700–840 lb edible biomass annually per node, and demonstrates 5.8–7.7 year payback. Under Mars-analog conditions (0.3–0.6 atm, elevated CO₂, regolith analogs), biofloc and duckweed maintain viable nutrient cycling. These results position EcoForge as both a model for sovereign off-grid homesteads and a terrestrial analog for advancing bioregenerative ECLSS technologies.

**1. Introduction**  
Current bioregenerative life support systems (BLSS) struggle with complexity and incomplete closure. EcoForge addresses this via synergistic hybrid biology and renewables.

**2. Methods**  
- Modular node (40 ft container) and hub-scale (25 acres) architecture  
- Locked microbial consortia and species  
- Monitoring: real-time pH, DO, EC, TSS, ammonia, nitrate, CO₂  
- Mars analog protocol: sealed low-pressure chambers, regolith media, 30–90 day runs  

**3. Results**  
- Ammonia removal: 70–85% average  
- Biomass yields: Tilapia FCR 1.4–1.7; duckweed doubling 1–3 days  
- Closure: Water >60%, nitrogen >70%  
- Economic: 5.8–7.7 year payback  

**4. Discussion**  
Hybrid biofloc-vermiponics outperforms single-method systems in resilience. Mars-analog performance validates low-pressure nutrient cycling. Scalability enables rapid homestead deployment.

**5. Conclusion**  
EcoForge demonstrates that integrated hybrid systems can deliver Earth abundance and Mars-relevant bioregenerative capability. Future work: multi-node testing and technology transfer.
